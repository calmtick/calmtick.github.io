Mindful Practices Handbook

  1. Find a space that ensures personal safety and is away from noise.
  2. Generally, choose a relatively quiet, well-ventilated indoor space. It's best if the place is comfortable in terms of temperature and humidity, and free from mosquito bites. If practicing outdoors, try not to do so alone.
  3. Sit cross-legged, maintaining an upright posture without leaning forward or backward.
  4. Maintain a straight and relaxed neck, tuck in the chin slightly without exerting force.
  5. Don't close your eyes completely; let your eyelids naturally lower with a slight gaze directed about 45 degrees downwards. Keep a gentle smile on your face.
  6. Start by taking deep breaths for a minute to stabilize your breathing rhythm. Afterward, don't intentionally adjust your breathing; let it return to its natural state.
  7. Don't deliberately visualize any scenes, nor try hard to eliminate thoughts. Let thoughts come and go in your mind; simply feel them without responding.
  8. Throughout this process, no matter how fascinating images and sensations appear in your mind, resist the urge to chase after them. These are just dreams of wakefulness; allow them to come and go.
  9. When you're done with the practice, slowly open your eyes, rise up, and leave the place.
  10. Don't share the scenes and sensations you experienced during the practice with other practitioners, so as not to lead others into an attachment of imitation and expectation, which may hinder self-aware enlightenment.
  11. It's advisable to set a daily practice goal for yourself to maintain your spiritual journey.

Wishing You Good Luck!
